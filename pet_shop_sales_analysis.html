Key Insights:

Top Performing Category: Dog Food accounts for $146,197.76 in sales (30% of total revenue).

Best-Selling Product: Premium Dry Dog Food generates $58,479.10 in sales (12% of total revenue).

Weekly Sales Pattern: Saturday is the highest-grossing day, while Tuesday has the lowest sales. Consider running promotions on slower days to boost traffic and revenue.

Seasonal Trends: July shows the highest sales, while February has the lowest. Plan inventory and staffing accordingly for these seasonal fluctuations.

Basket Size: The average transaction value is $39.12. Consider implementing cross-selling strategies to increase basket size.

Customer Loyalty: 68.5% of customers are repeat shoppers. Implement a loyalty program to increase customer retention and frequency.

Recommendations:
1. Inventory Optimization: Focus on maintaining optimal stock levels for top-selling products, especially Premium Dry Dog Food and other items in the Dog Food category.
2. Marketing Strategy: Increase marketing efforts during Tuesday and Wednesday to boost sales during slower periods.
3. Customer Retention: Implement a customer loyalty program with personalized offers based on purchase history to increase repeat business.
4. Cross-Selling: Train staff to suggest complementary products to increase average transaction value.
5. Seasonal Promotions: Plan seasonal promotions and product bundles to capitalize on peak selling periods and mitigate slow seasons.
